Human Development Index and Happiness Score

The Human Development Index (HDI) is a statistic created by the United Nations that evaluates the quality of education, the life expectancy and the per capita income of a country. This index ranges from 0 to 1: the higher the score, the more the country qualifies as "developed".

Similarly, the United Nations created another index: the Happiness Score of a country. This score relies on people's own rating of their lives.
